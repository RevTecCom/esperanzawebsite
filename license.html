Legalism: Useless in the face of the only "theft" that matters

Communism is against copyright, it is against the pride of the individual artisan, it is against both the attitude of petty-bourgeois idealogues and the defenders of large commodity capital towards copyright.

There is a rampant disease in the discourse around technology, with everyone trying to find the "perfect license" for their work, so that they avoid getting "scammed" by a large conglomerate. Some also approach it with the disgusting prideful attitude of hampering creative iteration, a petty-bourgeois attitude, akin to a chef who relies on a "secret recipe".

We have no compassion towards the latter concerns, and see only futility in the former concerns. The legal department of a large conglomerate can get away with essentially anything. If they want to take something, they will. Capitalism must necessarily turn private labor into social labor for the purpose of increasing profits, this is one of its primary objectives, and it will bend its own "rights" to achieve them.
